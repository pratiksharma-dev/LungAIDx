### Problem statement
Lung cancer and Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease (COPD) are leading causes of mortality worldwide, with patient outcomes severely hindered by late stage diagnoses. Current screening methodologies are often invasive (e.g., biopsies), cost prohibitive (e.g., CT scans), or overly reliant on single source data streams that fail to capture the full clinical picture. The core problem addressed by the Mednoia project is the fragmented nature of patient data, which prevents early, accessible, and highly sensitive detection. By creating a unified diagnostic profile that leverages principles from both biotechnology and advanced machine learning, this project holds the potential to democratize early respiratory screening. This approach can dramatically improve survival rates and reduce healthcare costs globally through an accessible, multi faceted risk assessment.

### Overall solution
The solution is a Multimodal Risk Prediction Engine that effectively fuses disparate patient data types structured clinical history, unstructured medical notes, and raw respiratory audio into a single, robust risk assessment model. This architecture heavily leverages recent advancements in foundation models to maximize diagnostic sensitivity. Google's Health Acoustic Representations (HeAR) model serves as the core acoustic feature extractor, processing digital stethoscope recordings to capture critical acoustic biomarkers like wheezes and crackles without requiring manual signal processing. Concurrently, MedGemma (2B or 9B parameter versions) is deployed to process complex clinical discharge summaries. It is prompted to extract nuanced risk factors such as occupational exposure or genetic family history and convert them into dense text embeddings. 

### Technical details
Built using PyTorch, the engine employs an intermediate (feature level) fusion architecture designed for high product feasibility and seamless cloud scalability. The pipeline processes independent data streams simultaneously: tabular clinical data undergoes ordinal encoding, scaling, and polynomial feature generation, while raw 2 second respiratory audio clips are converted into normalized log mel spectrograms. 

These spectrograms are passed through the frozen HeAR foundation model to extract 512 dimensional embeddings. To map complex correlations between a patient's clinical history and their specific acoustic biomarkers, the system extracts the dense hidden layer outputs from the base models. The 128 dimensional tabular features, MedGemma text embeddings, and 64 dimensional HeAR audio features are concatenated into a unified tensor. This fused tensor is then passed through a trainable Multilayer Perceptron (MLP) fusion head. To ensure clinical reliability in real world deployments where positive cases are rare, the network is trained using a weighted Binary Cross Entropy loss function to explicitly handle class imbalance, optimizing specifically for high Recall (Sensitivity) and ROC AUC scores.hf
